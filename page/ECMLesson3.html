Lesson 3: Behavioral Management Techniques

Responding to Challenges with Confidence

Behavior issues can arise even in the best-run classrooms. What matters most is how you respond to those issues. This lesson explores practical strategies to redirect behavior calmly and respectfully, so the focus stays on learning, not power struggles.

Stay Calm, Stay Grounded

The more frustrated you feel, the more calm you must appear. Use short, clear instructions. Speak privately when possible. Maintaining steady eye contact, a gentle touch on a desk, or a simple “check your choices” can go a long way.

Redirection Over Reaction

Instead of escalating, redirect. For instance:

  • Let’s try that again. How should we walk to the door?
  • I can tell you’re frustrated. Would you like to take a quick break or reset?

Teach Emotional Regulation

Build a classroom culture where students recognize their feelings and take positive actions. Introduce calm corners or use reflection cards. Teach strategies like deep breathing or counting to five.

Logical Consequences, Not Punishment

The goal isn’t to punish, but to restore order and teach better behavior. For example, if a student disrespects a group, they might apologize and rejoin after completing a short reflection.
